Signal is a material, not a decoration.

For a decade the industry treated expressive interfaces as the opposite of legible ones. We do not accept the tradeoff. The FRAME is deterministic; the SIGNAL is what moves inside it. Both are engineered.

Every component in SignalframeUX is a contract between the frame and the signal. Buttons hold their geometry while their labels tighten under load. Tables keep their columns locked while their cells push status colors up from underneath. A terminal readout scrolls at its own cadence, unapologetically, because that's the pace of the data.

This is not a stylesheet. This is a posture. Designers who ship under this system inherit its constraints — zero radius, no gradients, no stray opacities — and its one permission: to make a thing feel like it was made, on purpose, by someone who was there.

"If the frame is locked, the signal is free." — CD House Rule · 0003

Where other systems hand you a palette and ask you to be tasteful, SignalframeUX hands you an instrument. The tuning is fixed. The piece you play on it is yours.

The remainder of this dispatch walks through three applications of the type stack: a data density stress test, a machine-output readout, and the full scale ladder from micro-caption to display. None of these are decorative. Every one is in production.
