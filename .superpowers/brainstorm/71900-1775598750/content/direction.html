Three Directions for SignalframeUX

Not visual designs — strategic approaches to how the site performs for hiring managers + developers. Click the one that pulls you.

A

The Artifact

Reference: Detroit Underground release page, Rudnick type specimen

The site itself IS the proof. No "about" section, no explanations. You land on it and the design system is performing — the typography is alive, the SIGNAL layer is visible, the tokens are governing everything you see. Components aren't displayed in a grid — they're used in context, building the page you're reading. The site IS the component library.

Hiring signal: "This person doesn't explain their work — the work explains itself."

Risk: May be too opaque for cold visits. Developers may not find the docs.

B

The Architecture Exhibition

Reference: tDR portfolio, Stripe's design system docs

The site communicates the SYSTEM — the thinking behind every decision. FRAME/SIGNAL isn't a tagline, it's demonstrated with before/after: here's the page with FRAME only (clean, structural, boring), here's what SIGNAL adds (alive, tense, felt). The intellectual lineage is visible. Token decisions are traced to design rationale. Every component page shows WHY, not just WHAT.

Hiring signal: "This person thinks in systems and can articulate design decisions at the architectural level."

Risk: Could read as too cerebral. Needs visual impact to match the explanation.

C

The Controlled Detonation

Reference: tDR poster energy, Awwwards SOTD winner

The site hits you. Full-viewport hero with the GLSL shader dominating, not backgrounded. Typography at maximum tension (Anton at 200px+, not safely centered). The SIGNAL layer isn't subtle — it's the first thing you see and the last thing you remember. Components are shown but the experience IS the portfolio piece. Scroll is an event, not navigation. Every section transition is a designed moment.

Hiring signal: "This person ships at the highest visual craft level AND builds the systems underneath."

Risk: Could feel like spectacle over substance if docs/tokens aren't solid underneath.

Note: These aren't mutually exclusive. The final design likely combines elements. But which LEADS? Which feeling hits first when someone opens the URL?
